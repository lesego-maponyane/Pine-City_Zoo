GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, easily recognized by their long necks and legs, along with their distinctive brown and cream-colored spotted coats. They are herbivores, primarily feeding on leaves of trees, using their long necks and tongues to reach high branches. Giraffes live in Africa, primarily in savannahs and woodlands, and are social animals that live in groups called "towers

Tallest: They are the tallest mammals on Earth, with adults reaching heights of 16-20 feet. Long Neck and Legs: These long limbs allow them to reach high branches for food and run quickly.

Spotted Coat: Each giraffe has a unique pattern of brown and cream spots on its coat. Herbivores: They eat leaves and other plant matter, using their long tongues to gather food.
